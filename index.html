Colors

At Hobsons we're using two main colors - Hobsons Teal and Gray:

  • .bg-hobsons
  • .bg-gray

as well as four secondary colors - Fuchsia, Purple, Teal, and Green:

  • .bg-fuchsia
  • .bg-purple
  • .bg-teal
  • .bg-green

Our secondary colors are used primarily for accents. As such, they should never overpower our primary colors.

Shades and Tints

For situations when more or less contrast is required in order to establish proper hierarchies, each of our colors has a number of lighter tints as well as a darker shade.

.bg-hobsons150 .bg-offblack .bg-fuchsia150 .bg-purple150 .bg-teal150 .bg-green150
.bg-hobsons75 .bg-gray75 .bg-fuchsia75 .bg-purple75 .bg-teal75 .bg-green75
.bg-hobsons50 .bg-gray50 .bg-fuchsia50 .bg-purple50 .bg-teal50 .bg-green50
.bg-hobsons25 .bg-gray25 .bg-fuchsia25 .bg-purple25 .bg-teal25 .bg-green25
.bg-hobsons10 .bg-gray10 .bg-fuchsia10 .bg-purple10 .bg-teal10 .bg-green10

All SASS variables for colors can be referenced using the 'color()' mixin:

color(hobsons), color(gray), color(fuchsia), ...
color(hobsons150), color(offblack), color(green10), ...

There may be situations when real transparency works better than a predefined color. With SASS, we can use the built-in 'transparentize()' function instead of the predefined color variables:

transparentize(color(hobsons), 0.5)

Note: If you need the RGB or HEX values for all our tints and shades, these are defined in our master SASS and CSS files.

Footnotes and sidebars are set at 14px and always use Hobsons Gray.

Units

Our base unit is defined at 12px.

$base

Sizing all elements as multiples of our base unit helps maintain consistency and visual flow. The base unit also acts as the baseline for our typography. This helps with heading sizes and maintains a vertical rhythm.

Grid

Our layouts use a 12-column grid system that was built with our base unit in mind. Our page (viewport) has an inner double gutter on each side. This lets our content breathe and avoids it running into the sides of the page.

On large glass, each column is 72px wide with a 12px gutter. Adding the page's double gutters, we have a total width of 1200px.

On small glass, each column is 6% wide with a 1% gutter.

Aligning elements to our grid helps maintain visual consistency. Positioning elements outside of the grid should be done with extra care - it is a treatment usually reserved for highly important elements as it will make them stand out more.

Buttons

While most of our design elements are rectangular, all of our buttons share the same rounded form. They also share the same all-caps type. This helps them stand out and be easily recognized as actionable items.

Our buttons come in two flavors: solid and outlined. We can intermix them freely based on visibility requirements - solid buttons stand out more, outlined buttons blend better with the surrounding content.

Fuchsia is the primary color for calls to action - these stand out the most. As such, they shouldn't be overused.

Forms

All our form elements - fields and labels - share a common look. This makes them instantly recognizable.
The only exception to this rule can be one-off fields such as one-field forms or stand-alone controls - filters, checkboxes, radio buttons.

To keep our forms compact and easy to use on small glass, labels are positioned inside the fields. With a touch of javascript we can make sure this works as intended with pre-populated forms too.

Icons

Product icons

Product icons are a visual representation of our solutions and services. While each has a distinct meaning, they are all unified through concept and execution. We are using line icons with a consistent stroke width.
Their meanings has been documented in our Icons and Meanings document and icons from that standard set should be used whenever appropriate.

Interface icons

Sometimes our interfaces benefit from the use of icons as well. To differentiate these from our product icons, the interface icons are solid and typically much smaller in size. They are usually presented in our action color(s) when indicating an action area.

Our shared brand Dropbox folder contains a complete list of approved icons.

Logo icons

Our product logos contain graphic marks that, under certain conditions, can be used as standalone icons.

Use these as standalone icons only when the full branding and name of the product is present at a higher level. The standalone icon should never be the primary branding element. This is usually determined through positioning and size. The standalone icon should always be smaller than its presence in the full logo.

Standalone logo icons can only be represented in our approved list of colors: teal, gray, white.

Common scenarios include favicons and avatars.

Hobsons uses a different standalone icon than just the graphic mark in its logo. The same usage rules apply.

Animation

Transitioning between two visual states as the user performs an action acknowledges the action and establishes focus. With animations, however, more is often less. We use subtle effects to add a layer of visual delight. All transitions should be fast, smooth, and gradual. Regular easing is preferred for most except when additional focus is required, such as a popup or error message.

Design Patterns

Hobsons general design style is characterized by flat colors, bold contrasts, clear typography, and generous white space. We use aspirational imagery as a primary focal point, usually no more than one per section.

Overlays

Rectangular overlays using our brand colors are often superimposed on top of images. These can be either solid or use some sort of transparency and blending mode. A solid rectangle is always prefered when used in combination with body copy.

For added visual interest, our overlays can be staggered on top of images or other rectangular areas. We can do this symetrically or even asymetrically for more attention grabbing.
